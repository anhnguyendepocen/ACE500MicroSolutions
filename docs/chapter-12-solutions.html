Demand: \(Q = 1500 - 50P\)

\[C(q) = \frac{1}{2}q^2 - 10q +w\]

\[\begin{align*} MC(q) &= q-10 \\ p &= q-10 \\ q &= p+10 \end{align*}\]

\[AC(q) = \frac{q}{2}-10 + \frac{w}{q}\] Market supply can be expressed by:

\[Q= n*q = n(p+10)\]

So the market clearing condition is:

\[S=D\]

\[\begin{align*} n(p+10) &= 1500-50p \\ np+n*10 &=1500-50p \\ (50+n)p &=1500-10n \\ \end{align*}\]

Subbing in the entreprenuer condition \(n=0.25w\)

\[\begin{align*} (50+\frac{w}{4})P &=1500-\frac{10w}{4} \end{align*}\]

Now, from the no profit condition we know that the MC equals to the Average cost.

\[q-10=\frac{q}{2}-10+\frac{w}{q}\]

\[\frac{q}{2}=\frac{w}{q}\] \[q^2=2w\] \[q=\sqrt{2w}\] Then

\[w = \frac{(p+10)^2}{2}\] Now we have found two equations with only \(p\) and \(w\) in it. Two equations in two unknowns should be possible to solve.

\[[50 + \frac{(p+10)^2}{8}]p = 1500 - 10\frac{(p+10)^2}{2} \] Verify that \(P=10\) solves this equation. Then it follows that \(q=20\), \(n=50\), \(Q=1000\).
